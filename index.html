Reduce, Reuse, Recycle – these three 'R' words are an important part of sustainable living, as they help to cut down on the amount of waste we have to throw away.


It's Really simple!


Reduce the amount of waste you produce


Reuse items as much as you can before replacing them


Recycle items wherever possible.


Using the 3 'R's also helps to minimise the amount of space needed for landfill sites, where waste materials are disposed of.

Uncoated paper and cardboard can be composted, as can table scraps and spoiled food. I’ve used singe-layed cardboard from canned soda boxes to trace and cut out templates.


I once fashioned a circle punch out of a short length of steel electrical conduit and used it to punch circles out of old milk jugs, which I used as lid gaskets for small bottles.


Rust (iron oxide) can be heated red hot, cooled, and finely ground to make polishing rouge.


I make a LOT of small aluminum parts on my lathe, and that produced a LOT of shavings. I process these in a rod mill to make glittery aluminum powder for coloring plastic resins.


Similarly, if you cut a lot of wood with a power saw, you end up with a lot of sawdust. Mechanics are grateful for buckets full of sawdust, because it’s a lifesaver for oil spills.


Urban legends tell of some guy in Vermont who got so much junk mail, he rolled it into logs which he burned to heat his house for an entire winter.


Butt-ends and small bits of lumber can be burned as fuel, but if you know what you’re doing, you can make charcoal and wood tar from it.


Asbestos waste is a problem because it’s so dangerous, but someone finally tackled it. The solution was to grind up the asbestos, mix it into clay, mold it into bricks, then fire a glaze onto them, to entomb those asbestos fibers in the clay, where they won’t find their way into our lungs.
